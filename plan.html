🥩 What is Protein?

Let me break it down for you. Protein is the brick and mortar of your body. It builds your muscles, skin, nails—your strength.

It's not just that powder guys at the gym drink to get jacked. Protein is in everything from chicken and fish to eggs and even beans.

Protein keeps you full. If you're constantly hungry, chances are you're not eating enough protein.

Want to feel strong, satisfied, and take care of your body? Start by adding more protein to your plate. Every. Single. Day.

Chicken Breast

Lean, versatile and packed with protein — great for meal prep and low-fat diets.

Ground Turkey

Lower in fat than beef, high in protein. Ideal for tacos, bowls, and more.

Sirloin Steak

High-quality red meat with excellent iron and protein content. Enjoy grilled or pan-seared.

Lean Pork Chops

Great source of protein and zinc. Choose grilled or baked for a healthy meal.

Eggs

Affordable and versatile complete protein source. Great for breakfast or snacks.

Canned Tuna

Convenient and shelf-stable. High in protein and ideal for quick meals or salads.

Salmon Fillet

Rich in omega-3 fats and high-quality protein. Excellent for heart and brain health.

Protein Powder

Quick protein option post-workout or in smoothies. Choose clean-label options.

Ground Beef (90% Lean)

Great source of iron and protein. Ideal for meatballs, tacos, or bowls.

Tofu

Plant-based protein made from soybeans. Great in stir-fries, soups, or grilled.

Lentils

High in fiber and plant-based protein. Ideal for soups, salads, and veggie burgers.

Greek Yogurt

Thick, creamy and rich in protein. Great for breakfast, snacks or smoothies.

🍞 What are Carbohydrates?

Carbs are your body's main source of energy. They're not the enemy — they power your brain and workouts.

Think of carbs as fuel. Just choose the right ones like whole grains, fruits, and veggies — not the ultra-processed junk.

Balance is key. You don't need to fear carbs; you just need to respect their quality and quantity.

Quinoa

Complete protein grain with all essential amino acids. Fluffy texture, perfect for bowls and salads.

Brown Rice

Whole grain rice rich in fiber and B vitamins. Great base for stir-fries and meal prep.

Steel-Cut Oats

Heart-healthy whole grain that keeps you full. Perfect for breakfast or overnight oats.

Sweet Potatoes

Nutrient-dense root vegetable packed with vitamin A and fiber. Great roasted or mashed.

Whole Grain Bread

Look for 100% whole grain with minimal ingredients. Great for toast and sandwiches.

Buckwheat

Gluten-free pseudo-grain with earthy flavor. High in protein and minerals.

Pearl Barley

Chewy grain high in soluble fiber. Perfect for soups, stews, and grain bowls.

Whole Wheat Pasta

Higher in fiber than regular pasta. Pair with vegetables and lean protein.

Bananas

Natural energy source with potassium. Perfect pre or post-workout snack.

Mixed Berries

Antioxidant powerhouses with natural sweetness. Great for smoothies and yogurt.

🥑 What are Healthy Fats?

Fats get a bad rap, but your body needs them. The key is choosing the right kind — the ones that fuel your brain and fight inflammation.

Think of fats as your long-lasting energy source. They're in foods like avocado, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish.

Just watch your portions. Fat is dense in calories, but super valuable in a balanced diet.

Avocados

Creamy fruit rich in monounsaturated fats. Perfect for toast, salads, and guacamole.

Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Heart-healthy cooking oil with antioxidants. Great for cooking and dressings.

Raw Almonds

Protein-rich nuts with vitamin E. Perfect snack or salad topping.

Walnuts

Brain-healthy nuts rich in omega-3s. Great in oatmeal or as a snack.

Tiny seeds packed with omega-3s and fiber. Perfect for puddings and smoothies.

Ground Flax Seeds

Omega-3 rich seeds best absorbed when ground. Add to smoothies or yogurt.

Natural Almond Butter

Pure ground almonds with no added sugar. Great on apples or toast.

Coconut Oil

Stable cooking oil with medium-chain fats. Good for high-heat cooking.

Sesame Oil

Flavorful oil perfect for Asian dishes. A little goes a long way.

Olives

Mediterranean staple rich in healthy fats. Great for snacking or cooking.

🍎 Fruits & Vegetables

Eat the rainbow — different colors mean different nutrients. The more colorful your plate, the more nutrition you're getting.
